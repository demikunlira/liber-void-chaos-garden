Chapter One: The Return

The rain never stopped in this town. Isaac had forgotten that, or maybe he’d just made himself forget it, the way you make yourself forget the smell of a house you grew up in until you walk back through the door and it hits you all at once, low and sudden, like a hand closing around your throat.

He sat in his car across the street from Crimson Reel with the engine off and the wipers frozen mid-arc, watching the shop’s dying neon sign bleed pink and red into the wet asphalt. Half the letters had burned out years ago. It used to say CRIMSON REEL — VIDEO & MORE. Now it said something closer to CRI ON REE, which felt, absurdly, like the more honest name.

Closure, he thought. Right.

He said it out loud, actually, quiet and bitter, to no one. “Closure. Right.”

Ten years. Three months. He hadn’t counted the days, not consciously, but some old animal part of him had been keeping score the whole time, the way a scar keeps score even after you’ve stopped looking at it.
