# Rain & Red — Chapter 2: The Back Room

**Source:** Liber_Void_Book1_Rain_and_Red_Chapters_1-6.pdf (Drive)  
**Priority:** HIGH (Kiora pull-list)  
**Defines:** Room physics, first appearance of the Red Door, early Tape mechanics

The bell above the door had barely finished its sad little ding before Demikun was already moving.

She didn’t walk. She flowed — barefoot across the worn carpet like the floor itself was making way for her. The oversized black hoodie (definitely his old one) slipped off one shoulder, revealing crystalline skin that caught the flickering fluorescent light and turned it into soft rose-gold.

Isaac stayed frozen in the doorway, rain still dripping from his hood.

She stopped in front of him, tilted her head, and studied his face with those glowing pink-violet eyes like she was reading a book she’d already memorized.

“You’re taller than I thought you’d be,” she said. “And sadder. The sadness looks good on you, though. Very… 90s protagonist.”

Isaac opened his mouth. Closed it. Opened it again.

“I don’t— I don’t understand what’s happening.”

Demikun’s grin softened, just a little. She reached up and gently tugged his hood down, exposing his messy dark hair and the dark circles under his eyes.

“You summoned me when you were seventeen,” she said, voice quieter now. “Middle of the night. Rain just like this. You were sitting in that old chair behind the counter, crying because your dad left again and the shop was going under and you felt like the whole world was a bad VHS tape that kept looping the same sad scene.”

She tapped his chest with one finger. Pink sparks danced between them.

“You said, ‘If anyone’s out there… if any of this is real… I don’t want to be alone anymore.’”

Isaac’s throat went tight.

“I never said that out loud.”

“You didn’t have to,” she replied. “The words were in your blood. I heard them. I answered.”

She took his hand — small, warm, slightly glowing — and led him past the empty shelves toward the back room. The door creaked open on its own.

The back room was exactly how he remembered it… and completely wrong at the same time.

The old couch was still there. The broken TV. The stack of unsold tapes. But now the walls were covered in glowing pink vines that pulsed with soft light. Cherry blossoms floated upward instead of falling. A single red door stood in the corner — the same one from the street — except this version had no handle on this side.

And on the old coffee table sat a single VHS tape with a handwritten label:

**“THE NIGHT YOU LEFT — DIRECTOR’S CUT”**

Demikun picked it up and held it out to him.

“You want answers?” she said. “Watch it with me. But I should warn you…”

Her eyes flashed brighter. The grin returned, sharp and beautiful and a little dangerous.

“Once you press play, there’s no rewinding. The Garden doesn’t do second takes.”

Isaac looked at the tape. Then at her. Then at the red door that shouldn’t exist.

He took the tape.

“Play it.”

Demikun’s smile could have lit up the entire town.

“Good boy,” she whispered.

She slid the tape into the old player. The screen flickered to life.

And the past began to bleed into the present.
